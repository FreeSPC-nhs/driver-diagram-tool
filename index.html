Web-based driver diagram tool. Add an aim, primary/secondary drivers and change ideas, download as CSV, and export the visual diagram for reports. Colours and legend can be configured.

Use CSV to save your driver diagram and reload it later. The CSV includes the colour associated with each box.

      Help & information

      Using this tool

      This web-based driver diagram tool lets you build an aim, primary and secondary drivers, and change ideas, then export the diagram for reports or presentations.

      How do I add items?

      • Use 1. Add items on the left to enter the text and level.
      • Choose a parent if you already know what it should connect to (e.g. a primary driver for a new secondary driver).
      • Click Add item to add it to the diagram and table.

      How do I connect or re-connect boxes?

      • Use the round connector on the right of a box to add a new child at the next level (e.g. secondary driver under a primary).
      • Use the round connector on the left of a box to add or remove connections to items at the previous level.
      • You can have more than one connection into a box (e.g. a change idea linked to two secondary drivers).

      How do I edit text or colours?

      • Click on the text inside a box to edit it.
      • Click the small colour badge on the right of a box to cycle through the colour options you have defined.
      • Use 3. Colour options on the left to define or edit your palette.

      How do I change the look of the diagram?

      • Open 4. Diagram appearance on the left.
      • Adjust box height, vertical spacing, text size, font and boldness.
      • Click Apply appearance to update the diagram.
      • These settings are saved into the CSV so importing will restore your layout.

      How do I save and reload my work?

      • Use Download CSV to save a copy of the driver diagram (including connections, colours and appearance settings).
      • Use Upload CSV to reload a saved diagram and continue editing.

      How do I export for reports?

      • Use the header buttons Export chart (PNG) or Export PDF.
      • The export hides connector buttons and colour badges so you get a clean diagram.
      • You can also hide the legend and table first if you only want the diagram itself.

      About driver diagrams

      Driver diagrams are improvement tools that show how your overall aim is linked to the key factors that need to change, and the specific ideas you will test. They are widely used in quality improvement work across health and care.

      • Aim – the overarching goal you are trying to achieve, usually time-bound and measurable (e.g. “Reduce unplanned readmissions by 20% in 12 months”).
      • Primary drivers – the big components or system features that must change to achieve the aim (e.g. “Effective discharge planning”).
      • Secondary drivers – more specific factors that sit underneath each primary driver (e.g. “Clear discharge criteria”, “Timely follow-up”).
      • Change ideas – practical changes you can test that are expected to influence one or more secondary (or primary) drivers.

      You can read a driver diagram from left to right: if we implement these change ideas, they should influence our secondary and primary drivers, and together these should help us achieve our aim.

      The diagram can also act as a living document: as you learn from testing change ideas (for example using run charts or SPC), you can add, adapt or retire change ideas and update the diagram to reflect your latest thinking.
